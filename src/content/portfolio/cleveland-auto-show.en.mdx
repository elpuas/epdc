        ## The Challenge

        The *Cleveland Auto Show* had a site built on a custom CMS that had become outdated. Every update depended on rigid processes, maintenance was costly, and the *team had very little flexibility to manage content*, exhibitors, and events. On top of that, performance and the mobile experience were no longer up to the standard of an event of this scale.

        The challenge was clear: *modernize the platform without losing control*, improve speed, and give real autonomy to content editors.

        ## Our Solution

        As part of a *white label engagement for an agency in Ohio*, we rebuilt the site entirely on WordPress, using a *100% custom block theme* designed specifically for the event's needs.

        We implemented a *flexible block- and ACF-based architecture*, allowing the client's team to update complex sections without touching code. The new site delivers better performance, a strong mobile experience, and a structure ready to grow year after year, without relying on fragile custom development.

        The result was a **clean migration from a proprietary CMS to a standard, robust, and easy-to-maintain platform.**
