The *Phonogram and Single Management System* was developed as a WordPress-based application to model and manage structured data for the music sector. Rather than a conventional website, it was designed as an application layer that automates relationships, notifications, and complex administrative workflows.

        ## The Challenge

        The agency needed a solution capable of:

        - Registering phonograms and singles with multiple authors and performers.
        - Modeling dynamic relationships between works and the people involved.
        - Managing complex structures with nested repeaters.
        - Automating notifications by role (author, performer, administrator).
        - Generating structured exports compatible with internal processes.
        - Supporting bulk imports without compromising data integrity.

        This was not about publishing simple content, but about designing a robust system for handling critical information workflows.

        ## Our Solution

        As part of a *white label* project, we built a custom architecture based on:

        - Custom Post Types (`fonograma` and `sencillo`).
        - Advanced modeling with ACF and structured repeaters.
        - Gravity Forms + Advanced Post Creation.
        - REST API integration to query authors dynamically.
        - Automatic insertion of authors inside Gravity Forms repeaters through JavaScript.
        - Automated registration of relationships between each work and its participants.
        - A role-based notification system.
        - A custom export plugin.
        - Extension of WordPress’s native export system to adapt the output format to specific operational requirements.

        In addition, we developed a custom importer that supports structured uploads while preserving consistency across data relationships.

        The result was a stable internal application where WordPress operates as a data engine, not just a content management system.
